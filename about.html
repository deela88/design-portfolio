NICE TO MEET YOU

I’m Deanna! I’m a digital product designer from Orange County, CA. I’m here to enjoy the process of finding the best digital solutions to complex problems. Here’s why I do what I do.

When I was in the sixth grade, my aunt got me a subscription to Spin magazine, a big alt-music publication in the early 00s. Spin introduced me not just to the mindblowing powers of rock-n-roll and punk, but also amazing photography and graphic design.

I started making collages from my precious magazines, instinctively playing with text and layouts. I started taking commissions in the 7th grade. When the internet happened, I of course pirated Photoshop 7 and started offering layouts for my friends’ livejournals and MySpaces. In high school, I designed protest posters and pamphlets for Amnesty International.

It was at this point that I realized that design could communicate information that was outside simple text: emotion, style, tone. I don’t necessarily think that my design work could save the world, but I think it could make a difference in helping people tell their stories to the world.

As a graphic designer, I worked with visual elements that were designed to remain static. The challenge of UX/UI design is that the end product is intended for dynamic, sometimes unpredictable human input. How do I account for these changes? How could I help users make their decisions in the best way possible? Questions like these keep me going and gets me excited for the future of UI and UX design.

IS IT ME YOU'RE LOOKING FOR?

Hello! As a digital product designer grounded in 4 years experience with print design and production, I bring confidence in my ability and a passion for collaboration to the table. With a BA in International Studies, I have an academic history in research methodology and a knack for turning data into people-oriented solutions. It’s all about the “why” of design for me, but it’s also about “how” I can add to your team. Interested? Let’s chat.
